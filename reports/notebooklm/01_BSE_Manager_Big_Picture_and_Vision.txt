BSE Manager: Big Picture and Vision
===================================

What we are building
--------------------
BSE Manager is becoming a single operating system for the business, not just a dashboard.
The goal is simple but ambitious: one place where operations, accounting, forecasting, cash management,
contracts, and invoicing all tie together with auditability and decision-ready visibility.

Core outcome we are driving
---------------------------
We are replacing disconnected spreadsheets and ad hoc reconciliations with an integrated financial
control tower that answers these questions in real time:

1) What has happened? (actuals from QuickBooks and validated snapshots)
2) What is happening now? (hybrid current-month model with known invoices + manual budget controls)
3) What is likely to happen next? (project/phase revenue forecasts + contract/expense projections)
4) What does that mean for bank cash? (starting/ending balance rollforwards and reconciliation visibility)

The product direction in one sentence
-------------------------------------
From "recording transactions" to "running the business with confidence."

How this changes decision-making
--------------------------------
- Monthly close becomes more reliable because data quality checks and reconciliation are built in.
- Cash planning improves because bank rollforwards are explicit and linked to known drivers.
- Revenue planning improves because services projections happen at project and phase level.
- Expense control improves because contract labor and reimbursables are no longer mixed or opaque.
- Team communication improves because every number can be traced back to a source of truth.

What makes this system different
--------------------------------
- It uses QuickBooks as source data, but does not stop at bookkeeping views.
- It layers planning models on top of accounting reality.
- It separates historical truth from forward-looking assumptions cleanly.
- It keeps user-entered forecasts persistent and structured.
- It surfaces non-P&L cash movement so reconciliation is honest and explainable.

Strategic operating model
-------------------------
Historical months:
- Trust QBO snapshots and synced transactions.
- No retroactive guesswork.

Current month:
- Income uses practical hybrid assumptions (including near-term collections behavior).
- Expenses can be actively budgeted via manual line-item controls where needed.
- Bank ending balance can project from starting balance plus net income logic.

Future months:
- Services forecast at project/phase granularity.
- Contract labor projects from contract terms.
- Other expense lines use explicit budget entries, not hidden averages.

Why this matters now
--------------------
The company is at the stage where growth decisions need tighter control:
- Project mix and timing affect cash.
- Contract structures affect margin.
- Reimbursable capture affects realized profitability.
- Forecast quality directly impacts risk.

BSE Manager is being built to make that complexity manageable, visible, and actionable.
